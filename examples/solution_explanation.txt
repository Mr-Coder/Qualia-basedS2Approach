Water Tank Problem Solution

Given:
- Initial water volume: 5L
- Target water volume: 9L
- Ice cube volume: 200 cm³ per cube
- Ice cube addition rate: 1 cube/minute
- Water leak rate: 2 mL/second

Solution:
Step 1: Convert units and calculate net rate
- Ice cube volume in mL: 200 cm³ = 200 mL
- Volume added per minute: 200 mL/minute
- Volume leaked per minute: 2 mL/s × 60 s = 120 mL/minute
- Net volume increase: 200 - 120 = 80 mL/minute

Step 2: Calculate required volume change
- Initial volume: 5L = 5000 mL
- Target volume: 9L = 9000 mL
- Required change: 9000 - 5000 = 4000 mL

Step 3: Calculate time
Time = Required change ÷ Net rate
Time = 4000 mL ÷ 80 mL/minute
Time = 120 minutes (2 hours)

Therefore, it will take 120 minutes for the water level to rise from 5L to 9L.
